MAJOR DEPRESSION

If depressive symptoms persist for 2 weeks or more, your doctor may diagnose you with MAJOR DEPRESSION.

Major Depression looks different for most people but the following symptoms are common:

  • Loss of interest in activities.
  • Weight loss or gain.
  • Trouble getting sleep or feeling sleepy during the day.
  • Feeling restless and agitated or feeling sluggish.
  • Feeling tired and non-energetic.
  • Feeling worthless or guilty.
  • Trouble concentrating or in Decision making.
  • Thoughts of sucide

    • If these thoughts have crossed your mind, dial our suicide helpline; 020-314119

PERSISTENT DEPRESSIVE DISORDER

This is depression that lasts 2 years or longer. It involves dysthymia,a low-grade persistent depression, and chronic major depression.

Symptoms include:

  • Change in appetite.
  • Sleeping too much or too little.
  • Lack of energy.
  • Low self-esteem.
  • Trouble concentrating or making decisions
  • Hopelessness.

BIPOLAR DISORDER

This is where a person has mood episodes that range from extremes of high energy to low depressive periods. In the low phase, the individual exhibits symptoms of major depression.

SEASONAL AFFECTIVE DISORDER

A period of major depression that ahppens when one is not exposed to sunlight for long. It mostly happens to people living in countries that experience winter.

PSYCHOTIC DISORDER

This is where a person exhibits symptoms of major depression along with psychotic symptoms such as hallucinations and paranoia.

POSTPARTUM DEPRESSION

Women who have major depression in the weeks or months after childbirth are said to be having POSTPARTUM DEPRESSION.

As per research, 1 in 10 men also experience it.

PREMENSTRUAL DYSPHORIC DISORDER

Women with PMDD have depression and other symptoms at the start of their periods.

Symptoms include:

  • Mood swings.
  • Irritability.
  • Anxiety.
  • Trouble concentrating.
  • Fatigue.
  • Change in appetite or sleeping habits.
  • Feeling overwhelmed.

SITUATIONAL DEPRESSION

This is when you have a depressed mood when you're having trouble managing a stressful event in life. Some doctors call it "Stress-response syndrome"

Examples of stressful events are death of a loved one, losing one's job, divorce e.t.c.

ATYPICAL DEPRESSION

This is where a depressed person's mood is temporarily improved when a positive event occurs; the sadness is not persistant.

Symptoms include:

  • Increased appetite.
  • Sleeping more than usual.
  • Feeling heaviness in your arms and legs.
  • Oversensitivity to criticism.

TREATMENT-RESISTANT DEPRESSION

This is when an individual tries several treatment methods without success. The said person may have other conditions that make it difficult to treat the depression.

LIFESTYLE TIPS FOR WHEN YOU FEEL DEPRESSED

  1. Get exercise!
  2. Sleep well.
  3. Eat healthy.
  4. Avoid alchohol and drugs.
  5. Get some sunlight.
  6. Stay connected with people.
  7. Take time out for yourself regularly.
